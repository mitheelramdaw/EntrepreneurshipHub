Partnerships and Affiliations

In the current economic situation, it becomes mandatory for institutions to increase their networking opportunities through affiliations and partnerships. When partnerships are implemented correctly, the private sector can increase efficiency and choice, and expand access to education services, particularly for households that tend to be poorly served by traditional delivery methods.

Our overall aim is to create opportunities for young people to become active participants in society. To achieve this, we recognise that there needs to be a continuum of initiatives, starting from high school, moving to tertiary education and continuing through to young adult learning.

Amazon Web Services (AWS) is on a mission to accelerate the digital transformation of education in partnership with the full education community. Over 14,000 educational institutions use AWS to provide flexible, affordable technology solutions that support their core mission.

Students are learning anywhere, on any device, Schools are drawing on active, collaborative and entrepreneurial learning. This will asiist students in addressing real-world issues using skills such as critical thinking, problem solving and communication.

Work-integrated learning is incorporated into all programme offerings and requires appropriate workplace assessment for all students before the awarding of qualifications. External academic and industry representation on the Board Committees ensure that the content of programmes are constantly evaluated against industry and employer requirements and benchmarked nationally and internationally.

Full-time students complete a mandatory four-week internship at an agency, media or marketing company to gain real-life practical work experience. Many of our final year students receive employment offers even before they graduate. We provide opportunities for our students to interact with, and market themselves to people in the industry who matter. We invite the industry to our AAA showcase exhibitions, where they can view the student’s best work.
